Samsung Galaxy A55 Review — Best Mid-Range?

We used the Galaxy A55 as our daily driver for 60 days. Here's our completely honest verdict.

By GadgetPulse Team  ·  June 2026  ·  10 min read
← Back to Home

Samsung's Galaxy A55 sits at a tricky ₹37,999 price point — expensive enough that it competes with true flagships, but affordable enough to appeal to budget-conscious buyers. After 60 days of daily use, here's what we think.

📸 Camera Performance

The 50MP main camera with OIS is genuinely excellent in daylight. Photos come out sharp, well-saturated, and Insta-ready. In low light, Samsung's Night Mode does a commendable job — better than OnePlus at this price. The 12MP ultra-wide is decent but not impressive. Video tops out at 4K30fps which is standard.

🔋 Battery Life

The 5000mAh battery easily lasts a full day of heavy use — YouTube, Maps, Instagram, and calls. The 25W charging is the one real disappointment — competitors offer 67W at lower prices. Expect 90 minutes to full charge.

🖥️ Display

The 6.6-inch Super AMOLED FHD+ 120Hz screen is stunning. Colours pop, blacks are inky deep, and outdoor visibility is excellent. Best display in its price range, period.

⚡ Performance

Exynos 1480 handles everyday tasks, social media, and streaming flawlessly. Gaming on BGMI at high settings works well. Sustained performance under heavy load is where the chip shows its limits — it throttles after 30 minutes of gaming.

✅ Pros

  • Stunning 120Hz AMOLED display
  • Excellent daylight camera
  • 4 years OS + 5 years security updates
  • Premium glass build, IP67 water resistant
  • Clean One UI 6.1 experience

❌ Cons

  • Slow 25W charging vs competitors
  • Exynos chip throttles under gaming
  • Expensive at ₹37,999
  • No charger in the box
