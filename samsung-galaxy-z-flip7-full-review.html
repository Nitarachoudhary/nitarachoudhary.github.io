Samsung Galaxy Z Flip 7: Bigger Cover Screen, Real Daily Use Test

The 4-inch cover display is the headline change. We carried it for three weeks to see if it actually changes how you use the phone.

The Z Flip line has always been more about style than substance for a lot of buyers, but Samsung's decision to dramatically grow the cover screen on the Z Flip 7 is the first change in years that genuinely affects how usable the phone is day to day, not just how it looks.

📱 The New Cover Screen — What Actually Changes

At 4 inches, the cover display is big enough to type short replies, check notifications properly, and run a handful of supported apps without unfolding the phone. This is a meaningful step up from previous Flip generations, where the cover screen felt more like a smartwatch face than a usable display.

💡 What we noticed: Over three weeks, we found ourselves leaving the phone closed for far more of the day than with the Z Flip 6 — replying to messages, checking the time, controlling music — only opening it fully when we needed the main screen for something substantial.

📸 Camera Performance

The camera system is solid rather than spectacular — Samsung has prioritised the cover screen and chip upgrades this generation over a major camera overhaul. Daylight shots are good, low light is acceptable but not class-leading.

Camera & Display

  • Dual rear camera system
  • 6.7-inch main foldable display
  • 4-inch cover display (major upgrade)
  • 120Hz on the main display

Build

  • Familiar clamshell hinge design
  • IP rating for water resistance
  • New colour options for India
  • Compact when folded

🎨 Design & Build Quality

The hinge mechanism continues to feel solid and well-engineered — Samsung's years of iteration on this show. The phone is genuinely pocketable when folded in a way few other flagships manage, and the new colour options lean into the lifestyle-accessory positioning Samsung is clearly going for.

🔋 Battery Life

Battery life is the honest trade-off you make for the form factor. It comfortably gets through a normal day, but heavy users — lots of video, gaming, navigation — will want a top-up by evening. This isn't unique to the Flip 7; it's an inherent constraint of the compact folded battery size.

💰 Price in India

The Z Flip 7 sits below the Fold line, positioned as the more accessible — though still premium — foldable option. For buyers who want the foldable novelty and the lifestyle appeal without the Fold's price tag, this is the more realistic entry point into Samsung's foldable lineup.

✅ Pros

  • Cover screen upgrade genuinely changes daily usage
  • Compact, pocketable, well-built hinge
  • More accessible price than the Fold line
  • Strong lifestyle and colour appeal

❌ Cons

  • Camera system is good, not exceptional
  • Battery life requires a mid-day top-up for heavy users
  • Still a premium price relative to non-foldable flagships

⭐ Our Verdict

The Z Flip 7 is the most convincing case yet for the flip form factor as something more than a fashion statement. If the bigger cover screen is the thing that's kept you on the fence about foldables, this is the generation that addresses it directly.
