⚡ M5 Chip — 4x Faster AI

Apple claims that AI tasks on the MacBook Air M5 are up to four times faster compared to the M4 model, and much faster than the M1 version. The M5 features enhanced shader cores and a third-generation ray-tracing engine, improving gaming and 3D rendering. M5 also features faster unified memory with 153GBps of bandwidth — a 28% improvement over M4.

💾 512GB Base — Finally Enough Storage

The M5 MacBook Air now starts at 512GB of storage — double that of the previous M4 generation. Apple claims the new SSD delivers 2x faster read/write performance compared to the previous generation. This is a significant quality-of-life improvement — 256GB was genuinely too little for most users.

💡 For Indian users: 512GB base storage means you can store your full photo library, documents, and several projects without worrying about space. The M4 Air's 256GB base forced most users to constantly manage storage or buy expensive iCloud plans.

🖥️ Liquid Retina Display

The M5 MacBook Air uses the same excellent Liquid Retina display as the M4 model — no ProMotion yet (that's M6 territory per Apple's roadmap). But the existing display is gorgeous: 500 nits brightness, P3 wide colour, True Tone. ProMotion technology is not expected to arrive until at least the M6 MacBook Air.

🔋 18-Hour Battery — Real All-Day

Apple claims 18 hours of battery life on the M5 MacBook Air. In real-world testing with coding, writing, and video calls, expect 14-16 hours — still the best in its class. For Indian professionals working long hours without access to power sockets, this is a genuine advantage over Windows laptops in the same price range.

🆚 M5 vs M4 Air — Should You Upgrade?

✅ Pros

  • 512GB storage doubled from M4 base
  • 4x faster Apple Intelligence AI
  • 153GBps faster memory bandwidth
  • Wi-Fi 7 + Bluetooth 6 connectivity
  • Same great 18hr battery as before

❌ Cons

  • No ProMotion display (still 60Hz)
  • Same design as M4 Air
  • ₹1,19,900 starting price is high
  • 8GB RAM option no longer available

Should you upgrade from M4? Only if the storage upgrade or AI speed matters to you. Should you upgrade from M3 or older? Absolutely yes — massive performance and storage leap.
