WWDC 2026: The Good, The Bad, and The Ugly 🤠

Apple's biggest event of the year just happened. We cut through the hype and give you our completely honest take on what impressed us, what disappointed us, and what genuinely embarrassed Apple.

By GadgetPulse Team  ·  June 9, 2026  ·  7 min read  ·  Opinion

WWDC 2026 was a rollercoaster. Apple delivered some genuinely exciting things — and also served up some real disappointments. We watched every minute of the keynote so here's our honest, unfiltered verdict, no fanboy glasses on.

✅ THE GOOD

Things that genuinely impressed us at WWDC 2026.

1. Siri AI is finally, actually good

We've been burned before by Apple's Siri promises, so we were sceptical. But the demos at WWDC 2026 showed a Siri that genuinely understands context, remembers conversations, and acts across apps. Powered by Google Gemini, this is a real AI assistant — not a glorified web search. It's what Siri should have been three years ago, but better late than never.

2. The speed improvements are real and significant

30% faster app launches. 70% faster Photos loading. 5x faster external storage. These aren't marketing fluff — they're measurable gains. If you have an older iPhone, iOS 27 might genuinely feel like you got a new phone for free. This is Apple at its best: making existing hardware feel better.

3. They admitted Liquid Glass was too much

When iOS 26 rolled out last year with the controversial Liquid Glass redesign, Apple didn't budge despite user backlash. This year, they added a transparency slider and readability options. It's not a full reversal, but it shows Apple is capable of listening to user feedback. Small win — but a win.

4. iOS 27 supports all the way back to iPhone 11

Widest compatibility Apple has ever offered for a major iOS update. Usually Apple drops 2-3 iPhone models every year. This year, nobody gets left behind. If you're running an iPhone 11 in India — which many people are — you still get iOS 27. Respect.

5. Tim Cook's farewell was genuinely touching

Say what you will about Apple under Cook, but the man presided over the company's rise from $350 billion to over $3 trillion in market cap. His sign-off moment at WWDC 2026 was gracious and emotional. The end of an era, handled with class.

⚠️ THE BAD

Things that disappointed us or felt like missed opportunities.

1. No Hindi Siri AI — India gets left behind, again

Siri AI launches in English only. No Hindi. No Tamil. No Telugu. For a country with 600 million+ smartphone users, Apple's continued neglect of Indian languages is frustrating. Google Assistant and Samsung's Bixby both support Hindi. Apple's excuse of "more languages coming later" has been the same story for three years.

2. No iPhone Desktop Mode — still

Samsung, Google, and Motorola all let you connect your phone to a monitor and use it like a PC. Apple has been rumoured to be working on this for two years. WWDC 2026 came and went with zero mention of it. The leaks were completely wrong, and Apple clearly isn't ready. In an age where phones are as powerful as laptops, this feels like a deliberate omission to protect Mac sales.

3. Full Siri AI requires expensive hardware

Want the complete Siri AI experience with expressive voices and advanced features? You'll need an iPhone 17 Pro or Pro Max — phones that cost ₹1,30,000+. Most Indian iPhone users have older models. Apple is making its flagship AI feature a premium-only experience, which stings.

4. The event was too AI-focused, developers felt ignored

WWDC is supposed to be a developer conference — a week for app makers and programmers. But this year's keynote was almost entirely about Siri AI and Apple Intelligence, with barely a mention of new developer tools, APIs, or platform improvements. One reviewer called it "Apple forgot this was a developer conference." That's a valid criticism.

🚨 THE UGLY

Things that Apple should genuinely be embarrassed about.

1. Apple is paying Google $1 billion/year to power Siri

Let that sink in. Apple — the company that built custom silicon and the world's most advanced chips — is paying its biggest rival $1 billion per year to power Siri AI with Google's Gemini model. Apple spent years telling us privacy was a priority, then quietly licensed the most data-hungry AI company in the world to run inside your iPhone. The contradiction is staggering.

2. These AI features were promised in 2024 — two years ago

At WWDC 2024, Apple made big promises about Siri understanding your screen, acting across apps, and having personal context. In 2025, most of it didn't show up. Now in 2026, they're finally delivering. Two years of "coming soon" is not a good look for a company worth $3 trillion. Apple over-promised and massively under-delivered — and the tech press let them get away with it.

3. A protestor at Apple Park called out deepfake porn — and Apple has no answer

Outside the WWDC 2026 venue, a protestor criticised Apple through a microphone for not doing enough to fight AI-generated deepfake pornography. It's a real problem — AI tools are being used to create non-consensual intimate images of real people, and Apple's App Store has been criticised for not doing enough to prevent apps that enable this. Apple had no response. This is an ugly blind spot for a company that constantly markets itself around privacy and human dignity.

🎯 GadgetPulse Final Verdict

WWDC 2026 was a good event — not a great one. Apple delivered on Siri after years of broken promises, and the iOS 27 speed improvements are genuinely impressive. But the lack of Hindi support, the expensive hardware requirements for full AI features, and the awkward Google Gemini deal leave a complicated aftertaste. For Indian users, iOS 27 is worth the update — just don't hold your breath for Hindi Siri anytime soon.


Overall WWDC 2026 rating: 7/10 — Better than last year, but Apple still has work to do.
