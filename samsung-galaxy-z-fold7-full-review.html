Samsung Galaxy Z Fold 7: India's Best Foldable Phone in 2026?

200MP camera, a nearly crease-free display, and Galaxy AI fold-specific features. We tested it for three weeks in India.

Samsung's Galaxy Z Fold 7 is the company's most refined foldable to date. After years of foldables feeling like expensive experiments, this is the first one that genuinely feels ready for daily use — even if the price still puts it firmly in flagship-plus territory.

📐 Design — Thinner Hinge, Lighter Build

The Z Fold 7 is noticeably thinner than the Z Fold 6 when folded, and Samsung has redesigned the hinge to sit flatter, which removes most of the gap you used to feel when the phone was closed. It's lighter too — light enough that carrying it in a kurta pocket or a small bag no longer feels like a deliberate workout.

💡 Real-world note: After three weeks of daily carry — auto-rickshaws, crowded metro commutes, the usual Indian daily grind — the hinge showed no looseness and the outer screen held up well against minor pocket scratches.

🖥️ Display — The Crease Problem, Mostly Solved

The inner crease is still technically visible if you look for it at an angle under bright light, but in day-to-day use — reading, scrolling, watching video — it essentially disappears. This is the best Samsung has done on this front, and it's the single biggest quality-of-life improvement over the Z Fold 6.

📸 Camera — 200MP, First Time on a Fold

Samsung has finally brought its 200MP sensor — previously exclusive to the S25 Ultra — to the Fold line. This is a real upgrade, not a marketing number. Daylight shots are sharp with excellent detail, and the improved low-light performance is noticeable compared to the Z Fold 6's older sensor.

Rear Camera System

  • 200MP main sensor
  • 12MP ultra-wide
  • 10MP telephoto, 3x optical zoom
  • 8K video recording

Inner & Cover Displays

  • 7.6-inch inner foldable display
  • 6.3-inch cover display
  • 120Hz on both screens
  • Significantly reduced crease visibility

🤖 Galaxy AI — Fold-Specific Features

This is where the Z Fold 7 actually justifies its form factor rather than just being a phone that folds. Samsung has built AI features that specifically use the dual-screen, big-canvas nature of the device — split-screen translation where one half shows the original language and the other shows the translation in real time, and note-taking tools that use the large inner screen for handwriting-to-text conversion that feels genuinely useful rather than gimmicky.

🔋 Battery & Charging

Battery life comfortably gets through a full day of moderate-to-heavy use — browsing, calls, some video, messaging across both screens. Charging speed is competitive but not class-leading; Samsung still trails some Chinese brands on raw charging wattage.

💰 Price in India

The Z Fold 7 sits at a premium price point, positioned above the standard S25 Ultra and squarely in the territory where Samsung is betting buyers want the foldable form factor badly enough to pay extra for it. If you're coming from a Z Fold 5 or earlier, the jump in camera quality and display refinement makes a much stronger case for upgrading than the Z Fold 6 did.

✅ Pros

  • 200MP camera is a genuine upgrade
  • Crease is far less noticeable day to day
  • Thinner, lighter, more pocketable
  • Fold-specific Galaxy AI features feel useful, not gimmicky

❌ Cons

  • Still a premium price most buyers will hesitate at
  • Charging speed lags behind some competitors
  • Crease is reduced, not eliminated

⭐ Our Verdict

The Z Fold 7 is the most convincing argument yet that foldables are ready for mainstream use, not just early adopters. If you skipped the last two generations waiting for the experience to mature, this is the one that finally delivers on that promise.
