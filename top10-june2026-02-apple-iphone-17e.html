Apple iPhone 17e: The Budget-Friendly iPhone

Apple's most affordable current iPhone — A19 chip, MagSafe, and generous base storage.

The iPhone 17e is Apple's answer to buyers who want a current-generation iPhone experience without paying flagship prices — and the spec sheet suggests Apple didn't cut as many corners as past budget iPhones have.

⚡ The A19 Chip

Rather than shipping an older chip in its budget model, Apple has equipped the 17e with the same A19 chip family found in its current mainline phones — meaning performance shouldn't feel like a noticeably lesser experience compared to pricier siblings.

Key Specs

  • A19 chip
  • 256GB base storage
  • MagSafe compatible

Starting Price

  • $599 in the US
  • Positioned as Apple's budget entry point
  • Targets value-conscious iPhone buyers

💾 Storage & Value

A 256GB base storage tier is a notable value decision — previous budget iPhones have often shipped with smaller base storage that pushed buyers toward pricier tiers almost immediately. Starting higher means more buyers can comfortably stay at the entry price point.

🧲 MagSafe Support

Keeping MagSafe compatibility in the budget model means accessory compatibility — chargers, wallets, mounts — carries over from the rest of Apple's current lineup, which hasn't always been guaranteed on past budget iPhone models.

💡 Why it made the list: The 17e is notable less for breaking new ground and more for how few compromises it makes relative to its price — a current chip, solid base storage, and full accessory compatibility.

🤔 Who Should Buy It?

Buyers who want a genuinely current iPhone experience — not a recycled older model with a new name — at the lowest price point Apple currently offers.

⭐ Our Take

One of the stronger budget iPhone value propositions Apple has offered in recent years, and a clear standout among June 2026's notable launches.
