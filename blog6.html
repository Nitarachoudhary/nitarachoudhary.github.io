Travel Packing Tips

  • Always carry in cabin baggage. Power banks are not allowed in checked luggage on Indian flights — DGCA rules apply to all airlines including IndiGo, Air India, and Vistara.
  • Charge fully before departure. Don't assume you'll find a socket at the airport or on the train. Leave home at 100%.
  • Bring the right cable. Most phones now use USB-C, but many power banks still ship without cables. Pack both USB-C and Micro-USB if you're unsure.
  • Heat kills batteries. Don't leave your power bank in a hot car or direct sunlight. Rajasthan, Gujarat, and coastal Tamil Nadu summers can exceed 45°C in enclosed spaces.
  • One bank per person. For a family trip, buy one compact 10,000 mAh each rather than sharing one 20,000 mAh — it's more convenient and faster to charge.
